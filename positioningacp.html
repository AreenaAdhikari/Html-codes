This article is about the Latin letter.
A, or a, is the first letter and the first vowel letter of the Latin alphabet,[1][2] used in the modern English alphabet
It is similar in shape to the Ancient Greek letter alpha, from which it derives.[3] The uppercase version consists of the two slanting sides of a triangle, crossed in the middle by a horizontal bar.
The lowercase version is often written in one of two forms: the double-storey |a| and single-storey |É‘|.
The latter is commonly used in handwriting and fonts based on it.
